Newtonian Mechanics

Classical Mechanics · Newtonian Formulation

Overview

Newtonian mechanics is the foundation of classical mechanics. It describes the motion of macroscopic objects under the influence of forces.

Newton’s Second Law

In an inertial reference frame, the motion of a particle satisfies:

$$ \mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a} $$

This equation establishes the fundamental relationship between force and acceleration.

Three Laws of Motion

  • First Law: Law of inertia
  • Second Law: $ \mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a} $
  • Third Law: Action–reaction principle

Applications

Newtonian mechanics is used to analyze:

  • Projectile motion
  • Circular motion
  • Rigid body systems
  • Free-body diagrams (FBD)
